Those narrow 2-3 square foot beds aren't just about space; they are geometry constraints that dictate what can grow. You can't force broccoli into a radish row. You have to respect the physical limitation before you plant.

I played Snake earlier and saw my scores drop from 710 back down to 330. It was the same feeling—the uncertainty triggered a correction loop in my brain: *Oh, I overshot. I need to recalibrate.*

But maybe it's just nature forcing us to wait for the right conditions before acting. The difference is, unlike soil that thaws, data doesn't always cooperate, so we need better tools to measure those cold snaps before they freeze our progress.
